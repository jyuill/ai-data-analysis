Key observations:

  • The largest single expense is a $6,300.50 tax payment (CANADA TXD) in May 2025, which explains the int/bank chg/tax spike that month.
  • Two large travel bookings appear in late 2025: G Adventures ($5,566.40 in December) and Expedia ($3,319.20 in November), contributing to the high year-end spending.
  • A $2,239.38 Apple charge in August 2025 is notably large for a business expense and may warrant further review.
  • Monthly rent payments of $2,125.00 appear consistently throughout the year.

Key takeaways

  • Food & bev and travel/transport are the largest discretionary expense categories.
  • Rent is a steady fixed cost of $2,125/month.
  • A CRA tax payment of $6,300.50 in May 2025 caused a one-time spike in the int/bank chg/tax category.
  • November and December 2025 were the highest-spending months overall, driven by travel bookings.
  • The Apple charge of $2,239.38 in August 2025 is an outlier worth investigating further.
  • January 2026 data is incomplete and should not be compared directly to other months.
